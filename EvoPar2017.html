A performance assessment of evolutionary algorithms in volunteer computing environments:

the importance of entropy

Merelo, de las Cuevas, García-Sánchez, García-Valdez

This is an example of Drip Painting by a completely unknown author. In this type of painting structure and, if you will, beauty, emerges from the fractal nature of the dripping and how it distributes itself, apparently randomly, over the canvas. The technique, besides, allows people with not too much experience to contribute to art. In a volunteer way, maybe, such as the volunteer computer system we are working with. The fractal analysis of Pollock drip painting is old, but has been sufficiently proved and, in fact, the dimensions increased during his career.
Pat Steir is an artist that uses often this drip painting technique, introducing chance in her painting and giving them this natural look, as in the case of these Sixteen waterfalls. How structure emerges in the lower reaches of the canvas via this waterfall technique, by mixing one waterfall with another, reminds us how the power of this painting is not simple aggregation, but the mixing and interaction with whatever was on the canvas before that. In fact, the Chinese word for evolutiona> is the same word as for flow, as in a river, as in dripping. It's endless change, and in this paper we will see that evolution is helped by this creation of complexity, not only by aggregation.

Architecture of a volunteer system

The system uses a REST architecture for a pool-based system that allows interaction with the pool via simple HTTP commands, withouth any kind of hurdle and just by visiting a web page.

Time-to-solution vs. entropy

We are using compression entropy here, that is, compressing the string and measuring the ratio. Ratios close to 1 mean that strings do not have repeated patterns, while lower ones mean exactly the opposite. Clearly time decreases with entropy, not with the number or PUTs or the number of different IPs, although obviously more IPs will contribute to a higher IP if they contribute in a different way.
